01 — Empathize

Research & Discovery

The Problem

University students living independently face the perfect storm of budget pressure, time scarcity, and decision fatigue all at once. Through interviews and surveys, we found it wasn't just one thing — it was the collision of multiple painful realities hitting at the same time.

Key Pain Points

  • Cooking and buying groceries for one is difficult and food waste adds up fast
  • Rising grocery costs make it hard to stay within budget
  • Too many recipe options feel overwhelming, not helpful
  • Finding deals across multiple stores takes time students would rather spend elsewhere

Research Methodology

Survey — 32 Responses

Designed to gather bulk, representative quantitative and qualitative data from a diverse group of students. Results were used to focus and sharpen the subsequent user interviews.

  • Common challenges, strategies, and grocery budgets across a diverse student group
  • Mix of quantitative (budgets, frequency) and qualitative insight (priorities, frustrations)
  • Results directly informed the focus areas for user interviews

User Interviews — 6 Participants

Conducted with potential users to go deeper than survey data alone. Open-ended questions allowed conversations to go where the data pointed.

  • 6 interviews with students matching our target demographic
  • Open-ended questions encouraged rich, unscripted responses
  • Deeper, more focused conversations that revealed real mental models

Refined Design Question

"How might we make it more cost effective and time efficient for university students to plan meals and source relevant grocery deals?"

User Story

"As a university student, I want to efficiently find deals and recipes I can make cheaply so I can save time and money."

03 — Ideate

Brainstorming & Architecture

Bulk Ideation

Each team member independently generated ideas against the design question. Affinity diagramming surfaced categories — and revealed a surprise cluster: community-oriented features like shared lists and peer recipe sharing.

💡 Unexpected insight

Analogous Inspiration

  • Uber Eats — browse many options without feeling lost
  • Spotify — recommendations that feel curated to you
  • Tinder swipe — fast, low-effort triage of suggestions
  • Stock market — track price history to spot the best moment

Ideation Categories

  • Deal / coupon consolidation
  • Generative recipe suggestion
  • Smart list building
  • Community-driven services
  • Grocery store finding
  • Budget tracking

04 — 05

Prototype & Test

Phase 1 — Paper Prototyping

Free-wheeling and individual. Each team member sketched their own design vision with varying levels of fidelity. Goal was quantity over quality — surface as many ideas as possible, then identify the best elements across all versions.

Phase 2 & 3 — Figma Prototyping

Designs consolidated into a single shared Figma prototype. All major structural decisions locked in, page-to-page connections mapped. Phase 3 focused on refining small details — icon placement, heuristic compliance, interaction polish.

Reflection

What I Learned

✓ What worked

  • ↗Big-picture ⇌ small-picture design thinking cycles kept us from getting lost in detail too early
  • ↗Analogous inspiration (Uber Eats, Spotify) gave us strong mental models to build from
  • ↗Iterative testing caught usability issues we genuinely hadn't anticipated
  • ↗Bidirectional navigation emerged as a real differentiator from existing apps

→ Next time

  • ⤳Get user feedback earlier — tangible interfaces triggered insights early research didn't surface
  • ⤳Lock the design question before ideation — the first session was constrained by too-narrow framing
  • ⤳Build a functional prototype earlier for more realistic testing
  • ⤳Explore community features more — they emerged late but showed genuine interest

Next Steps

Develop a functional prototype for multiple design-test-build-learn cycles. Explore social recipe sharing between friends, improved deal-to-recipe matching, and a home screen widget for the generated grocery list to support in-store navigation.
